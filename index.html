Methods & standards

How our carbon is calculated — and how it becomes ACCUs

Transparent, NGER / IPCC-aligned, and built to plug into the real Australian carbon-credit scheme. This page is what we show to auditors, processors and the Clean Energy Regulator.

Mechanism

The carbon emission mechanism

Total emissions = Σ ( activity × emission factor )

Three gases → one unit (CO₂-e)

CO₂ ×1  — diesel, electricity
CH₄ ×28 — cattle & manure
N₂O ×265 — fertiliser & manure

Methane dominates cattle carbon — a little counts as a lot of CO₂-e.

Cradle-to-farm-gate LCA · Scopes 1·2·3

We count on-farm and embedded supply-chain emissions, then express them per unit of product (kg CO₂-e / L milk or / kg beef). Sorted into Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (energy) and Scope 3 (feed & transport) — the standard used by processors and lenders.

Why it's more than a calculator

Normal calculator vs LCCIP (LCA)

A normal footprint calculator

  • On-farm emissions only — stops at the fence
  • Ignores embedded feed & fertiliser emissions
  • Gives one lump total
  • No land / vegetation carbon
  • No credit pathway

LCCIP — life-cycle assessment

  • Cradle-to-farm-gate boundary (stated)
  • Counts embedded Scope 3 (feed, transport)
  • Reports intensity per litre / per kg
  • Includes land sequestration & land-use change
  • Shapes reductions into ACCUs (baseline − project)
Honest status. LCCIP produces audit-ready, NGER/IPCC-aligned data to support carbon projects. It does not itself issue ACCUs — certification requires an approved CER method, baseline and independent verification.

Why horticulture is different

Your footprint is mostly upstream

Unlike livestock, horticulture's biggest emissions aren't from the paddock — they're in packaging, fertiliser and freight. Scope 3 is 60% of the network total.

Packaging

Energy & fuel

Freight & water

Methods & standards

How horticulture carbon is calculated

Transparent, NGER / IPCC-aligned, cradle-to-farm-gate. This is the page we show to retailers, exporters and auditors.

Mechanism

The emission mechanism

Net emissions = Σ ( activity × emission factor ) − soil & biomass removals

Three gases → one unit

CO₂ ×1 — diesel, electricity, lime & urea
CH₄ ×28 — organic waste breakdown
N₂O ×265 — nitrogen fertiliser in soil

Soil N₂O is horticulture's hidden giant — 17% of network emissions from a gas 265× stronger than CO₂.

Boundary & functional unit

Cradle-to-farm-gate. 12 sources across Scope 1 (fuel, soil N₂O, refrigerant), Scope 2 (grid electricity) and Scope 3 (packaging, fertiliser upstream, freight, water, chemicals, planting materials, waste). Removals are reported separately, not netted into scopes. Output: kg CO₂-e per kg marketable yield.

Transparency

Factor provenance — what you can defend

Honest status. 111 factors: NGA 2025 and IPCC 2019 where available, proxies flagged. 67 are proxies requiring replacement before formal reporting. Removals require site-specific measurement (soil sampling or biomass modelling) to be creditable.
